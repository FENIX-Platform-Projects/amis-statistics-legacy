General

  • AMIS Commodities: Wheat, Maize, Rice and Soybeans.

  • Unit of measure: thousands (000) of metric tonnes unless otherwise stated.

  • AMIS estimates and forecasts are based on official and unofficial sources.

  • Unless otherwise stated, all charts and tables refer to AMIS data sources.

  • Estimates of world imports and exports may not always match, mainly because shipments and deliveries do not necessarily occur in the same marketing year.

  • All totals are computed from unrounded data.

  • Estimates for China also include those for the Taiwan Province, Hong Kong SAR and Macao SAR, unless otherwise stated.

  • Up to 2006 or 2006/07, the European Union includes 25 member states. From 2007 or 2007/08 onwards, the European Union includes 27 member states.

  • ‘-‘ means nil or negligible.

Brief Glossary


Commodities:

Total cereals: include wheat, coarse grains (maize, barley, sorghum, oats, rye, millet and other minor grains not elsewhere specified) and rice (in milled equivalent).

Maize: includes yellow and white maize.

Wheat: includes soft and durum wheat.


Supply and Demand Balances:

Total Supply= Domestic Availability + Imports


Domestic Availability = Opening (or carry-in) Stocks + Production


Opening (or carry-in) Stocks

Quantity of stocks at the beginning of the marketing year (carried-in from the previous marketing year). Opening stocks consist of supplies held at all levels within the food system; both by governments and the private sector (incl. farm holdings). Opening stocks of a given marketing year are always identical to the closing stocks of the preceding year.


Production

Production refers to the full amount of the harvest before any deductions are made for post-harvest losses, seed use, etc.

For cereals, production data refer to the calendar year in which the entire harvest or the bulk of it took place. For soybeans, production refers to a split year that brings together crops harvested in the latter part of the first year shown (normally applying to the northern hemisphere) and crops harvested in the early part of the second year shown (normally applying to the southern hemisphere).

Production of wheat, maize and soybeans is in terms of product weight. For rice, paddy is converted to milled equivalent.


Imports

Imports include commercial purchases from abroad as well as food aid deliveries. They are estimated/reported on the basis of:

  • National Marketing Year (or National Marketing Season): a twelve month period starting with the harvest of the main crop of the season.

  • Global Trade Year (or Global Trade Season):

    • Wheat and maize imports are calculated using a July/June trade year (conventionally referred to as the marketing season).

    • Rice imports are calculated using January/December over the second year shown.

    • Soybeans imports are calculated using October/September.

Wheat import data include wheat flour converted into wheat equivalent. The National Marketing Year import data may also include other processed products which are directly consumed as a “basic food”, such as semolina and bulgar. Nevertheless, these products are excluded from the Global Trade Year imports.

Rice imports are in milled terms.


Total Utilization= Domestic Utilization + Exports + Closing Stocks


Domestic Utilization

Includes food use, feed use and other uses.

  • Food use: refers to direct human consumption.

  • Feed use: refers to the quantities fed to livestock.

  • Other uses: include seed, industrial use and post-harvest losses .

    • Seed: quantity used for the planting of the following production cycle.

    • Industrial use: refers to products intended neither for direct human consumption nor for feed. It includes the manufacture of secondary food products such as starch, sweeteners and alcohol.

    • Post-Harvest Losses: include losses incurred after harvest, from sorting, waste, storage, transport, packing, etc.

For soybeans, the breakdown differs from that used for cereals; to reflect this, the relevant balance sheets are currently under modification.


Exports

Exports refer to all shipments outside the country, including food aid. They are estimated/reported on the basis of:

  • National Marketing Year (or National Marketing Season): a twelve month period starting with the harvest of the main crop of the season.

  • Global Trade Year (or Global Trade Season):

    • Wheat and maize exports are calculated using a July/June trade year (conventionally referred to as the marketing season).

    • Rice exports are calculated using January/December over the second year shown.

    • Soybeans exports are calculated using October/September.

Wheat export data include wheat flour converted into wheat equivalent. The National Marketing Year export data may also include other processed products which are directly consumed as a “basic food”, such as semolina and bulgar. Nevertheless, these products are excluded from the Global Trade Year imports.

Rice exports are in milled terms.

Closing (or ending or carry-over) Stocks

Quantity of stocks at the end of the marketing year (before the following year’s harvest) held at all levels within the food system, both by governments and the private sector (incl. farm holdings and households). Closing stocks of a given marketing year are always identical to the opening stocks of the following year.


Per Caput Food Use

Calculated as food use divided by population and expressed in kg per year.


Auxiliary Data


Planted Area (000 ha): refers to the area seeded over the crop year.

Harvested Area (000 ha): refers to the area harvested.

Yields (Kg/ha): refers to the whole grain yield obtained at harvest time net of losses incurred during harvesting and threshing. Usually calculated per unit of area harvested.

Population (million): population within the geographical boundaries of a country at the mid-point of the reference period. Refers to the number of individuals actually partaking of the food supplies during the reference period, including refugees.
