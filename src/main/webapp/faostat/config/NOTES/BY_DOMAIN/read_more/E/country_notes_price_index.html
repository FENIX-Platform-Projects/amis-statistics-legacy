Afghanistan:
All prices are consumer prices.

Albania:
Figs, Oranges, Tangerines, Mandarines, Clementines, Satsma, Lemons and Limes, Grapes, Apples, Pears, Apricots, Cherries, Peaches and Nectarines, Plums are mixed wholesale and producer prices.
Chestnuts, Soybeans, Sunflower seed, Olives, Tomatoes, Onions and Shallots, Onions, Garlic, Carrots, Leeks and other alliac.veg, Cauliflower, Cabbages, Lettuce, Cucumbers and Gherkins, Peas, Beans, Eggplants, Chillies and Peppers, Spinach, Pumpkin, Squash, Gourds, Watermelons, Cantaloupes and other Melons are wholesale prices.

Sheep meat: Lambs.

Argentina:
Prices of Sweet Potatoes, Cassava, Asparagus , Pumpkins, Squash, Gourds , Chillies & Peppers, Bananas , Grapefruit, Peaches, Plums, Strawberries, Watermelons, Cantaloupes are wholesale prices.

Armenia:
Prices of wine, table wine, quality wine are consumer prices.

Australia:
The annual prices reported are the weighted average Gross Unit Values (GUV) for the financial year. (GUV is defined as the total gross value of production of the commodity, at wholesale prices received in the market places, divided by the total reported production quantity).
Grapes: Wine grapes only (major component of total grapes production).
Animal Live Weight: values estimated based on the average ratio of conversion of each category of animal to meat produced, as advised by the meat industry.

Austria:
Prices of Gooseberries, Hops, Tobacco, Wool and Duck Meat, Geese Meat are based on Unit Values from the Economic Accounts of Agriculture.
Oilseeds nes: Pumpkin-seed for Oil.

Azerbaijan:
Azerbaijan changed data collection method and revised all time series since 2003. Collected data are consumer prices.

Bangladesh:
Prices refer to crop year and not the calendar year. E.g. 1999 prices refer to the 1998/1999 crop year.

Barbados:
All prices are wholesale prices.

Sheep: Price of Sheep and Goats.

Belgium-Luxembourg:
Tobacco: prices do not include direct subsidies on the product, as per the countries' new method.

Brazil:
Brazil changed currency twice in the period 1991-2001.
Nuts nes: Pinhão (Pine Nut)
Rapeseed: Canola
Oranges : from 1993 to 1997 price of Oranges is the average of prices from FGV. Since 1998 it is the average of prices from IEA-CEPEA market.

Natural Gums: Sorva

Bulgaria:
A new methodology to conduct survey for collecting agricultural producer prices was introduced in 2007. This methodology focuses on the price of the produce intended for direct human consumption and which is sold at considerably higher prices. Hence, producer price data from 2007 onwards could be higher than in the previous years.
All fruits and vegetable prices are unit values.
Pimento, All spices: Price of Herbs.
Cattle: Price of Beef and Buffaloes up to 2000, from 2001 only cattle.
Buffalo : Price of Beef and Buffaloes up to 2000, from 2001 only buffalo.
Sheep: Price of Sheep and Goats up to 1999, from 2000 only sheep.
Goat Live Weight: Price of Sheep and Goats up to 1999, from 2000 only goats.

Burkina Faso:
Sorghum: in 2004 Price of Sorghum is the average price of white sorghum and red sorghum.

Burundi:

Vegetables fresh nes: “Irengalenga”
Bananas: average prices of “ Ibigurube ” and “ Utumaramasenge ” varieties from 2002 onwards, prices of ripe bananas until 2001.
Plantains: green bananas.
Fruit nes: “ Maracudja ” and “ Prunes de Japon ”.
Coffee : Robusta only.

Croatia:
Croatia declared its independence in 1991. Price data are available from 1994 onwards due to the war that started in 1991 and subsequent hyper-inflation. Data were compiled and made regularly available after the introduction of the new currency, the Kuna, which took place in 1994.
All data are unit values.
Mixed grain: including sorghum, millet, buckwheat and other cereals.
Oranges and mandarins: from 1994 until 1997 oranges and mandarins we collected together.
Watermelons: including musk melons.

Cyprus:

Vegetable fresh nes include beetroots and celery.

Hungary:
Producer prices are provided as procurement prices.
